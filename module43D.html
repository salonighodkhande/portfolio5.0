Day 1: Basics of 3D Printing

Objective: Learn what 3D printing is, understand its types, and study printer components.

Details: 3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, builds objects layer by layer using digital files. The most common beginner-friendly type is FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling). Key printer parts include:

  • Frame: Provides stability and structure.
  • Print Bed: Surface where objects are built.
  • Extruder & Nozzle: Melt and deposit filament.
  • Filament: Printing material (like PLA).
  • Stepper Motors: Control precise movements.
  • Control Board: Acts as the brain of the printer.

Activities: Watched demo videos of FDM printers, explored how digital designs transform into physical objects.

Day 2: Materials & Safety

Objective: Learn about filaments, bed adhesion, and essential safety rules.

Details: Different filaments are used depending on strength, flexibility, and durability:

  • PLA: Easy to use, eco-friendly, good for beginners.
  • ABS: Strong but needs ventilation (fumes).
  • PETG: Balance of strength and flexibility.
  • TPU: Flexible, rubber-like material.

Bed Adhesion: Methods include tape, glue sticks, build plates, and heated beds to prevent prints from warping.
Safety: Always handle hot nozzles carefully, ensure good ventilation, and avoid touching moving parts.

Day 3: Introduction to CAD & STL Files

Objective: Learn to design 3D models and understand STL files.

Details: 3D models are created using CAD software like TinkerCAD (beginner-friendly), Fusion360, or Blender. The designs are exported as STL files, which store the 3D surface geometry of an object. These files are universal for most 3D printers. Beginners can also download free models from sites like Thingiverse or Printables to practice.

Activities: Designed a simple keychain in TinkerCAD, explored STL file structure, and downloaded sample models.

Day 4: Slicing Software

Objective: Understand slicing and prepare STL files for printing.

Details: A slicer converts STL files into G-code, which contains printer instructions. Popular slicers include Cura and PrusaSlicer. Key settings include:

  • Layer Height: Affects resolution and print time.
  • Infill %: Determines strength and weight of the print.
  • Supports: Temporary structures for overhangs.
  • Print Speed & Temperature: Must be optimized per material.

Activities: Practiced slicing a model, previewed toolpaths, and adjusted infill density for testing.

Day 5: First Print & Calibration

Objective: Calibrate the printer and execute the first test print.

Details: Calibration includes bed leveling and adjusting nozzle height. A well-leveled bed ensures proper adhesion of the first layer. Test prints like calibration cubes or Benchy boats are commonly used. Issues like stringing, under-extrusion, or warping can be identified during the first test.

Activities: Calibrated the printer manually, ran a Benchy test print, and monitored first-layer adhesion.

Day 6: Troubleshooting & Print Quality

Objective: Identify problems and improve print quality.

Details: Common issues include:

  • Bed Adhesion Issues: Fix by re-leveling or adjusting bed temperature.
  • Stringing: Reduce by lowering temperature and enabling retraction.
  • Layer Shifting: Caused by loose belts or unstable bed.

Activities: Experimented with slicer settings, tested prints at different speeds and infill, compared results.

Day 7: Post-processing & Advanced Learning

Objective: Learn finishing techniques and explore advanced 3D printing.

Details: Post-processing includes:

  • Sanding: Smoothens surface.
  • Painting: Adds aesthetics.
  • Acetone Smoothing: Works for ABS prints.

Advanced Filaments: Wood-fill, carbon-fiber, and TPU expand possibilities. Multi-material and resin (SLA/DLP) printers provide higher detail and flexibility.

Activities: Printed a custom phone stand, applied sanding and painting, explored advanced materials.

Learning Outcomes

  • Use slicing software independently for any model.
  • Operate advanced 3D printers with touch and remote interfaces.
  • Plan a print from start to finish with minimal supervision.
  • Understand the connection between digital design and physical prototyping.
