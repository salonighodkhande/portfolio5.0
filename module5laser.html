Day 1 – Introduction & Applications

Objective: Understand the fundamentals of laser cutting, types of lasers, common applications, and safety basics.

Details: Laser cutting is a technology that uses a laser to cut materials precisely. Key laser types include CO2 lasers (for wood, acrylic), Fiber lasers (metal), and Diode lasers (small-scale cutting). Applications include decorative items, signage, prototypes, and educational models. Safety is paramount: always wear protective glasses and ensure proper ventilation.

Activity: List 5 items that can be made using laser cutting.

Day 2 – Hardware & Software

Objective: Learn about laser cutter components and design software.

Details: Important parts include the laser tube, bed, mirrors, exhaust, and control panel. Software like LaserCad allows you to design shapes and send jobs to the machine. Understand the difference between Vector cutting (cut outlines) and Raster engraving (fills) and file formats like .svg and .dxf.

Activity: Design a simple shape using the software.

Day 3 – Design Basics

Objective: Learn how to prepare designs for cutting and engraving.

Details: Decide whether the job is cut or engrave. Use press-fit design tips for assembling multiple pieces. Maintain appropriate kerf and spacing to ensure accurate fitting. Understanding vector paths and proper file setup is essential for precision.

Activity: Design a small box with engraving on top.

Day 4 – Materials & Settings

Objective: Understand material types and machine power/speed settings.

Details: Materials include wood, acrylic (transparent, colored), paper, cardboard, and more. Proper power and speed settings ensure clean cuts without burning or charring. Conduct test cuts to determine optimal parameters for each material.

Activity: Run a speed/power test grid on cardboard to find optimal settings.

Day 5 – Operating the Laser

Objective: Learn operational skills to safely run a laser cutting job.

Details: Focus the laser correctly, set the bed height, check air assist and exhaust. Ensure the file is correctly loaded and the machine is ready. Perform safety checks including emergency stop awareness before starting the job.

Activity: Cut your first real design.

Day 6 – Project Day

Objective: Apply skills to create a functional project.

Details: Prepare files, use nesting to optimize material usage, assemble parts after cutting. Students choose a project like a stand, box, or 3D design. Emphasis on precision, alignment, and finishing.

Activity: Complete and document your project.

Day 7 – Review & Maintenance

Objective: Learn maintenance, troubleshoot common issues, and present projects.

Details: Common issues include burn marks or incomplete cuts. Perform basic maintenance such as cleaning lenses, mirrors, and the bed. Showcase and reflect on projects to reinforce learning.

Activity: Present final work and reflect on learning experience.
